ELEPHANT

loxodonta africana

African elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth, playing a crucial role as ecosystem engineers in their habitats. These magnificent creatures shape landscapes by creating water holes, dispersing seeds, and maintaining savanna grasslands. Unfortunately, their populations have declined drastically due to ivory poaching and habitat loss. Their complex social structures, led by matriarchs with decades of knowledge, are essential for herd survival during droughts and migrations.
