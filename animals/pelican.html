SPOT-BILLED PELICAN

pelecanus phillipensis

The spot-billed pelican, with its distinct gray-brown plumage and characteristic bill marked with dark spots, is a majestic waterbird found across southern Asia. Once widespread, its numbers have sharply declined due to wetland destruction, pollution, and overfishing that strips away its food supply. Today, their strongholds survive only in scattered pockets, mostly in India and Sri Lanka, with global populations under strain. Without continued conservation of wetlands and protection from human pressures, the spot-billed pelican risks sliding irreversibly toward extinction.
